## Overview

The Campaign Planner turns the Campaign from plumbing into the DM's **authoring-and-running workspace** for the two things the app doesn't yet own: **the passage of campaign time**, and **a living world that changes as that time passes.** Combat tracks turns; dungeons track dungeon turns; nothing tracks **days** — and nothing records that the world moved while they did. The rulebook is explicit on both: time is a resource and "*a campaign moves on whether you do or not*" (§2.2); growth happens in downtime (Sparks, Collaborator bonds, §1.2/§2.3).

**Scope — the planning half.** A DM lives in two states: **planning** (between sessions — authoring the world and the near future) and **playing** (at the table, in a live mode). The existing features already cover the *playing* episodes — combat has the Encounter console, the delve has the Dungeon console — and the third playing mode, the live **scene/social** cockpit (negotiation, downtime at the table, talking to NPCs), is its own forthcoming sibling PRD. *This* PRD owns the other state and the connective tissue: the **planning surface** plus the **spine** every mode writes to — the clock, the world, and the Chronicle. The episodes are discrete; the spine runs between them.

Two pillars over the Campaign, meeting at two moments:

- **The Campaign Clock** — an advancing in-game **day counter** whose days carry ordered **slots**. A slot is either a **story beat** (a prepped narrative scene the DM runs live, sourced from **Session Notes**) or **downtime** (the empty state — each placed character records one activity). The clock also carries DM-authored **deadlines** that count down and optional day flavor (**season**, **events**). Authored ahead and run live: one record, two moments.
- **The Living World** — a web of authored entities, each accreting a **timeline**. **Articles** are soft, label-typed worldbuilding entries (Settlement, Faction, Culture, …); **NPCs** are a distinct, structured kind (Identity/Origins shape + an **Arcana**, an Archetype **Lineage**, and a party-wide **bond** — every trait but the name filled in progressively). They're wired together by **directed relations** and each accrues **updates** — day-stamped events that compose into the **Chronicle**: an overall world timeline and a per-entity timeline.

**The two pillars meet at two moments.** At the **downtime slot**, a **Collaborator** activity advances an NPC's bond (which opens that Lineage in the party's Atlas). At **day-end**, the DM runs the capture ritual: review what the party did, then author what the *world* did — *"the world moves whether you do or not,"* made literal.

**The spine has two temporal faces.** The **Calendar** looks *forward* (upcoming days, scheduled beats, counting-down deadlines); the **Chronicle** looks *back* (the accreted world timeline). Same spine, opposite directions — the DM plans in one and remembers in the other.

**Mechanical rewards are player-owned.** The clock screens record *what happened* (narrative); they never hand out Sparks, Talents, or gold — players tap those on their own character sheet. **The clock writes nothing to a character's mechanical state.** The **one exception** is the **NPC bond**, and it is not a character write: a bond is DM-side **world state** (the NPC's tier), confirmed by the DM, that later gates the Atlas as a read-model *input*. Everything else the app "adjudicates" (7 Sparks → a Virtue; practice → a Talent) is the *character sheet's* own concern, driven by the player — out of this feature's scope.

**Two architectural commitments shape everything below, both yours:**
- **Composition over inheritance — at two levels.** *Between kinds:* "has a timeline" and "is relatable" are **composed capabilities**, not a base class. An Article, an NPC, and a PC are distinct, properly-shaped entities that each *compose in* the relation/timeline behavior — the same component-composition you use in game-v2, applied to the authoring layer. Relations and updates point at a **participant** (anything that composed the capability), never at "an Article." *Within a kind:* an NPC's own content (name, Identity traits, Lineage, Arcana, bond) is a set of **progressively-composed traits** — **name is the only required one**; the rest fill in as the NPC earns depth. A quick-minted "Ferryman" is a legitimate NPC; the structured traits are enhancement, not an entry gate.
- **World Anvil's structure, done right.** This is World Anvil's article + relationship web — but a World Anvil article is a static page, and yours **accretes history**. The timeline is the differentiator, and it's only possible because you own a clock. Articles stay deliberately thin (a name, a type label, a little prose) and earn their depth by accumulating updates, not by filling form fields.

## Goals

- Give every Campaign a **Campaign Clock**: an advancing **day counter** whose days carry ordered **slots**, each slot either a **story beat** or **downtime** — authored ahead and run live (one record, two moments).
- Let the DM author prep in **Session Notes**: an Obsidian-style folder tree where **folder = session, note = beat**. A beat is a prepped narrative scene with prose + entity links, scheduled to a slot, **floating** ("run anytime"), or unscheduled.
- Let the DM **run a day** in the **Day Runner**: walk its slots in order, run story beats, resolve each character's downtime, and adapt live via **Defer / Run** (defer a beat to the shelf; pull a floating beat into a downtime slot).
- Let the DM **record downtime** as free narrative tagged by category (Virtue / Talent / Practical / Collaborator) with linked "concerns" — **recording only**, never awarding a mechanical reward. (A reusable, typed activity *library* is a **P2** nicety, not v1.)
- Let the DM author **deadlines as dated Articles** (Looming → Due → Resolved) that count down, **hard-gate** the day they come due until resolved, and **resolve** into a ⚑ Chronicle marker — never auto-resolving the fiction (§2.2); the dated Article is the event/threat, its agents linked.
- Give the DM a **Calendar**: an upcoming-only agenda of days ahead, a multi-deadline countdown ribbon, add-days controls, per-day **season** (inherit-forward) and **events** (a dated Article, `kind: event`), and beat-scheduling into open slots.
- Run the **Day-End Capture** ritual: review the party's recorded activities, author **world updates** (tag a participant, write it, link what it concerns), and advance the clock — with **pre-suggested** updates for resolved beats, fired deadlines, and bond-tier crossings.
- Give the DM a **Chronicle**: a first-class past-facing surface — the aggregate world timeline — the temporal twin of the Calendar; per-entity timelines live on entity pages.
- Let the DM author **Articles** — soft worldbuilding entries with a **label-only type** and free prose — quick-createable inline as a name-only stub.
- Let the DM author **NPCs** — a distinct, structured kind reusing the PC **Identity/Origins** shape, plus an **Arcana**, a **Lineage**, and a party-wide **bond** — with every trait but the name filled in progressively, quick-createable inline as a name-only stub.
- Wire entities together with **directed relations** (display on the source; "bidirectional" writes the reverse edge), each with a free-form label.
- Give every entity a **timeline of updates**: at **day-end**, the DM tags a participant, writes an update, and links the other participants it **concerns**; the update lands on each of their timelines and composes into the Chronicle.
- Make advancing an NPC's **bond** open that Lineage in the Atlas, **tier by tier**, for the whole party — a party-wide bond gating *availability* while **acquisition still costs each PC their own Saved Ranks**; **opt-in per campaign**, **non-breaking** otherwise. (Bond mechanics activate only once the NPC has a **Lineage**.) A character's **own Origin Lineage** rides the campaign-wide **story tier** instead — the party's shared arc opens your innate gift (FR-15).

## Non-Goals (v1)

- **Any player-facing surface.** v1 is **DM-only / private** — no player watch of the clock, deadlines, beats, Articles, NPCs, relations, or updates. The visibility model (how many reveal switches: participant / relation / update) and the redacted player view are a **deferred milestone**, intentionally not designed yet.
- **The live scene/social play mode (DM cockpit).** Running negotiations, downtime at the table, and freeform scenes *live* is the **playing** counterpart to this **planning** PRD — a **separate, forthcoming sibling PRD** (the social/scene equivalent of the Initiative Tracker and Dungeon Map). Its likely headline is a **Negotiation tracker** (§2.3: Interest/Patience, Motivations/Pitfalls — the social analog of the combat tracker). This PRD provides the **spine** that mode draws on (the clock, NPCs, the Chronicle), not the cockpit itself.
- **Awarding or mutating any character mechanic.** The clock **records narrative and writes nothing to a character** — no Sparks, no Talents, no gold, ever. The player owns and taps those on their own sheet. The activity's category tag is *categorization only* (Chronicle color); only **Collaborator** has a mechanical effect, and that effect (the bond) is **world state**, not a character write.
- **A reusable, typed downtime-activity library (P2).** v1's downtime is a **free-text composer** — one prose activity per character per slot, tagged by category, with linked concerns. A DM-authored library of reusable activities (name, slot cost, solo/party-wide, requirement) is a **nice-to-have deferred to P2**, not a launch requirement.
- **Party-wide downtime as a data shape (accepted trade-off).** A downtime slot holds one entry *per character*; there is no "the party did X together, write once, attach to N." Party-wide activities are **generally story beats** (campaign-wide by nature), so party-wide *downtime* is expected to be rare — a DM who wants it records the same activity per character. Revisit only if real play shows it's common.
- **Live mechanical consumers for the Virtue/Talent/Practical tags (accepted trade-off).** In v1 only the **Collaborator** tag is mechanical (the bond); the other three are **inert categorization** (Chronicle color) — their consumer is the deferred player surface. Accepted: this is a personal/small-group tool, not designed for outside users, so inert tags are fine.
- **A standalone planning dashboard.** Cut. The four surfaces each carry their slice of the briefing (Day Runner = the day + party + progress; Calendar = the days ahead + deadlines; Chronicle = the past; Day-End = the ritual). A synthesizing "needs-attention" home is redundant and not built.
- **Per-type field schemas.** An Article's type is a **pure label** (filter + icon); no code or schema keys off it. Per-type structured fields (a Settlement's population, a Religion's tenets) are the World-Anvil form-chore and are out. NPCs are the one kind with real structured fields — which is exactly why they're a distinct entity, not an Article type.
- **A full game-v2 ECS substrate for authoring.** "Composition" here is realized at the **feature/data layer** (shared relation + update tables keyed by a generic participant `{kind,id}`, plus concrete per-kind entity tables) — *not* by forcing Articles/NPCs into game-v2's entity/component persistence. (The technical design decides the exact split.)
- **NPC-as-combat-enemy / statblock persistence.** An NPC's combat statblock **rides the game-v2 entity-persistence cutover** (which doesn't exist yet). v1 NPCs are social/narrative; the table **reserves the seam** but carries no statblock.
- **Story-beat auto-logging.** Resolving a beat writes nothing to the Chronicle on its own — a beat is *prep* (possibly branchy), not history. What happened is captured by the DM authoring a world update at day-end (which the resolved beat **pre-suggests**).
- **Behavioral coupling to Dungeon / Travel / Combat.** The DM **claims slots for a dungeon** ("Run a dungeon," FR-5) — a planner-side link — but the coupling is one-directional and manual: the dungeon console **never advances the clock**, dungeon status never auto-resolves a slot, and "a delve takes both slots" is **never enforced** (§2.2's "consumes the rest of the day" is the default fiction, not a law — claim one slot, claim the next if the party runs long). Travel stays "advance N days"; combat encounters get no slot link in v1.
- **A named fantasy-calendar engine.** Day counter + optional free-text labels (rename slots, tag the season, drop an event) only — no months/weekdays/leap math. Real calendars are hard enough; fantasy ones are out.
- **A travel subsystem.** Hex travel/methods/complications (§2.4, itself unwritten) are out; travel is "advance N days."
- **Automated world simulation.** The Chronicle never invents or advances an update — the **DM authors every one**; the app prompts (at day-end), links, and renders.
- **A dice roller / random tables.** Deadlines and reminders nudge; the DM rolls.
- **Hard global Lineage gating.** Gating is opt-in per campaign; characters outside a campaign, or in a non-gating one, keep today's all-open Atlas.
- **Players picking their own downtime.** DM-driven in v1.

## Users & Context

The sole user in v1 is the **DM**, working in two modes against one campaign-scoped record: **prep** (author the world — Articles, NPCs, relations, beats — and the next stretch of time — deadlines, seasons, events) and **run** (advance the day, run beats, resolve downtime, capture updates at day-end, watch deadlines tick, advance bonds). On a laptop or tablet, at or between the table. **Players have no surface in v1** (their visibility is a deferred milestone). Because the clock writes nothing to a character, there is **no player-adjacent write** at all: every write in this feature is campaign-scoped and gates on `requireCampaignDM`. The clock *reads* character state (Virtues, Sparks x/7, Talents, level/archetype on the downtime card) but never writes it.

## Navigation & Surfaces

The Campaign *is* an icon-rail toolset (there is no separate dashboard). `/campaigns/[campaignShortId]` resolves to the **Day Runner** — the home surface, and the one that becomes the live play surface. A shared **icon rail** links the surfaces; **Manage Campaign** (gear, pinned bottom) routes to the existing campaign admin (invite link, roster, encounters, archive/delete).

The rail's surfaces:

- **Day Runner** — run a single in-game day: the slot rail + the downtime-resolution workspace + Defer/Run + End-the-day.
- **Session Notes** — author prep: the folder tree (sessions), the beat editor, the schedule control.
- **Calendar** — plan ahead: the upcoming agenda, the deadline ribbon, add-days, seasons/events, beat-scheduling.
- **Chronicle** — look back: the aggregate world timeline (per-entity timelines live on entity pages). *(To be designed before build; behaves as described here.)*
- **Articles** — author soft, label-typed worldbuilding entries. *(Unmocked; derived from the entity-linking patterns.)*
- **NPCs** — author the structured NPC kind. *(Unmocked; same.)*
- **Manage Campaign** — admin (relocated off the old jammed campaign page).

**Day-End Capture** is not a rail destination — it is the ritual the Day Runner's "End the day" opens.

## Architecture & Integration

New **DM-only surfaces inside the existing app**, on/under the campaign page (`/campaigns/[campaignShortId]`), reusing the app's auth, campaigns, and progression engine. Full technical design (storage realization, day/slot materialization, the relation/update model, the Atlas seam, the game-v2 dependency) is deferred to a **technical design session**; this section states the load-bearing *product* decisions.

**Author and run are one record — no template/instance split.** Unlike a **Map** (a reusable template snapshotted per run, because one map serves many parties), a campaign's clock and world are **never reused across parties** — they *are* this campaign. So there is no snapshot: the authored plan and the live state are **one campaign-scoped record**, edited before play and mutated during it.

**The Campaign Clock: day-scoped ordered slots, kind derived.** §2.2's three scales are combat turns (Encounter), dungeon turns (Dungeon), and **the day** (slots, travel-days, deadlines). The clock owns the third and never reaches into the others. A **day carries ordered, named slots** (default **Morning → Evening**; the DM can rename them and add more, e.g. "Night"). A slot's **kind is not stored — it is derived**: a beat scheduled into the slot makes it a **story beat**; a **dungeon claimed** into it makes it a **dungeon slot**; otherwise it is **downtime** (the implicit empty state — there is no distinct downtime slot *type*). This is "decide the distinction once": the beat's `schedule` and the dungeon claim resolve the slot's kind for every surface. **Story and dungeon slots are campaign-wide** (one scene or delve, one "resolved" check); a **downtime slot holds one free-text activity per placed character**. There is **no spanning model** — an activity does not consume multiple slots or span days or fan out party-wide as a data shape; "takes both slots" / "several days" is handled narratively (write it that way, add a slot, or advance N days). **Day counter + optional labels**, relative math, no calendar engine.

**Beats are prep, not participants.** A beat (= a Session Note) composes **neither Relatable nor Timelined**. It *references* participants (the entity chips in its prose, the same linking vocabulary as the composer) but does not itself sit in the relation web or accrue a timeline. A beat carries one **`schedule`** — `{ day, slotId } | 'floating' | 'unscheduled'`. **Folders ("sessions") are purely organizational** — they group beats and map to *nothing* on the clock; a session is beats composed together, not a linear script (branching lives in the prose). Beats can be born in Session Notes *or* inline via "Run story beat → New story beat" (an inline beat lands in a default **Unfiled** session, never orphaned).

**Defer / Run is live adaptation.** On a story slot, **Defer** unschedules the beat to the **floating shelf** (`schedule → 'floating'`), and the slot reverts to downtime. On a downtime slot, **Run story beat** pulls in a floating beat (or a new one); if that slot already has recorded downtime, a confirm sets it aside (kept, reversible). Deferral only ever happens because the DM chose it (see the day-end warning) — the app never auto-defers.

**Capabilities are composed, not inherited.** Two behaviors are written **once** and composed onto distinct entity kinds:
- **Relatable** — participates in the relation web.
- **Timelined** — accrues updates (its timeline).

The entity kinds that compose them: **Article** (one table, a label `type`), **NPC** (its own structured table), and the existing **Character/PC** (referenced, not modified). Relations and updates reference a **participant** = `{ kind, id }` (`kind ∈ { article, npc, character }` in v1). The web/timeline machinery is blind to *kind* — adding a future kind is a new `kind` value + an authoring surface, **never a schema or render change** (CLAUDE.md §9). Realized at the **feature/data layer** — *not* by an inheritance hierarchy and *not* (in v1) by the game-v2 ECS persistence substrate.

**Articles are thin, label-typed, soft entities.** An Article = name + a **type** (curated set — Settlement / Faction / Culture / Organization / Religion / Event / Location / Item / … — plus DM-defined custom) + free prose, composing Relatable + Timelined. The type is **pure label**: it filters, groups, and picks an icon; **nothing keys off it**, so custom types are free and safe. An Article is **quick-createable inline** from the composer/linker as a name-only stub (name + default type, no prose) — a stub the DM fleshes out later on the Articles surface.

**NPCs are a distinct, structured kind whose traits compose progressively.** An NPC reuses the PC **Identity/Origins** shape (ancestry / backstory / personality / hopes / dreams / fears / secrets + knives / chains) and adds NPC-only traits: a Major **Arcana** (from the Arcana Toolkit — its narrative soul), an Archetype **Lineage** (the mechanical gift; the stable 12-key union in `packages/game-v2/src/kernel/vocab/lineage.ts`), and a party-wide **bond**. **Every trait but the name is optional and filled in progressively.** An NPC is thus **quick-createable inline** as a name-only stub (the DM's-worst-nightmare case: the party talks to an unplanned "Ferryman," you mint him from the linker in two seconds, the update lands on his fresh timeline, and he exists when they come back tomorrow). A stub NPC is a **valid narrative participant immediately** (relations, updates, timeline) — it simply has no Arcana/Lineage/bond yet. **Collaborator mechanics degrade gracefully:** with **no Lineage**, the bond/Atlas-gating affordance does not appear (a random ferryman shouldn't gate anyone's Atlas); assigning a Lineage lights the machinery up. There is **one NPC kind**, not a "quick" vs "full" split (that would re-introduce a distinction to migrate across when a stub graduates). A **"stub" badge/filter** on the NPCs surface makes under-built NPCs findable — the DM's "flesh these out" worklist. The **statblock/Entity side is a reserved seam** (null in v1). "Collaborator" is not an entity — it is the **bond + Lineage-gift aspect** of an NPC.

**Relations are directed edges; direction is display ownership.** A relation is `source → target` with a **free-form label**, and it **displays on the source's page**. "Bidirectional" is not a symmetric type — it is a convenience that **also writes the reverse edge** (`target → source`, with its own label), so it shows on both. This solves hub clutter: a kingdom page shows only the 10 settlements *it* points at, while 50 NPCs can each point *at* the kingdom from their own pages without crowding it. Relations are part of the **static web** and are authored deliberately.

**Updates are the timeline unit; they echo only along their own links.** An update is **day-stamped**, authored on a **primary participant** ("tag this NPC/Article"), with free text and a **"concerns" set** of explicit participant links. It appears on the primary's timeline **and** each concerned participant's timeline. The **static relation web does *not* propagate updates** — otherwise a hub like the kingdom would drown in every settlement's and NPC's updates. So both the web and the timeline stay curated, each by a different deliberate act. The **Chronicle** is the aggregate: the **world timeline** (all updates) = a first-class surface; a **per-entity timeline** (updates where that entity is primary-or-concerned) = on the entity's page. (Direction is irrelevant for updates — an event "concerns" its participants equally.)

**One primitive absorbs the sources; the Chronicle's only auto-source is downtime.** A player's downtime activity **auto-writes an update** on that character's timeline; a **Collaborator** activity writes one that *concerns* the NPC, so it lands on both timelines for free. Every *other* Chronicle entry is a **DM-authored world update**, written at day-end. There is no separate "change log" vs "activity log" — just updates. **Resolving a story beat writes nothing** on its own (a beat is prep, not history); instead it **pre-suggests** a world update at day-end.

**The day-end ritual is the seam between Clock and World.** "End the day" is a review step: it surfaces the day's player activities (already updates) and **prompts the DM** to author world updates — tag a participant, write it, link what it concerns — then advances the day (firing any due deadline nudge). The prompt is a **nudge, not a gate**. Four things **pre-suggest** an update (pre-filling the composer, never auto-writing): a **resolved beat** (pre-tags its primary participant, pulls its entity chips in as concerns, seeds the draft from its tagline), a **fired/looming deadline** (pre-tags its threat participant, if linked), and a **bond-tier crossing**. Advancing is guarded by a **hard gate** for any Due deadline (above) and then a soft **warning** (below) for beats/downtime.

**Advancing is reversible — scoped.** "Advance" is not a point of no return: the DM can **un-advance** (roll the clock back one day), which returns to the prior day (its downtime entries never moved) and re-opens any deadline resolved on the day being undone — the resolution note survives as an ordinary update, unbound from the anchor. Un-advance does **not** reverse the day-end warning's bulk beat actions (Resolve All's marks; Defer's unscheduling) — the confirm says so plainly — but any **deferred beat remembers the slot it left**, so the floating shelf offers one-click "return to Day N · Slot." Deadlines recompute for free (pure selectors); un-advance exists for the fat-finger and "the party hadn't actually finished" cases.

**The day-end warning (anti-forgetting).** "End the day" stays visually muted until every slot in the day is resolved, then brightens — the anti-forgetting cue. If the DM ends the day while any story beat is unresolved **or** any placed character is missing a downtime entry, a warning fires with three choices:
- **Cancel** — stay on the Day Runner; nothing changes.
- **Resolve All** — "it all happened, I just didn't tick": mark every unresolved **story beat** *and dungeon slot* *resolved*; **fill open downtime with Idle** (below); advance.
- **Defer Unresolved Beats** — "we didn't get to those scenes": send every unresolved **story beat** to the **floating shelf** and **unclaim** unresolved dungeon slots (the delve didn't happen; re-claim it tomorrow); **fill open downtime with Idle**; advance.

Both proceed-buttons handle empty downtime the same way: each missing character gets a one-click-equivalent **Idle** entry (a character genuinely may do nothing some evenings — Idle is honest where hard-blocking would force junk prose). They differ only in **beat/claim treatment** (resolved vs floated/unclaimed). **The app never auto-defers** — deferral happens only because the DM clicked it.

**Deadlines and Events are *dated Articles* — one dated-facet, two kinds.** A deadline is not a standalone construct with an optional threat link; it **is an Article that carries a date**. An **Article may optionally be *dated*: at most one `{ day, kind: 'event' | 'deadline' }`** — a facet of the Article itself, 1:1 with its date (not a join, not a collection). Undated Articles (Saltmere, the Ashen Pact) stay plain worldbuilding; dating is optional and orthogonal to the type label (a dated Article is usually type `Event`). The **dated thing is the event/threat, never the agent**: "**The Eclipse**" is the deadline (a dated Article); "**The Demon King**" is a **linked participant** (a concern/relation), never the dated thing. NPCs are **not** datable — an NPC is a persistent agent with many potential dated events, which is exactly the "which deadline?" ambiguity this forbids; the event ("The Inquisitor's Arrival," "The Siege of Saltmere") is Article-shaped and the agent gets linked. **Multiple related deadlines = multiple dated Articles linking a shared agent** (the Demon King's relation web shows The Eclipse → Day 17, The Ritual → Day 24, each detailed on its own Article page) — one-anchor-per-Article is what keeps each deadline detailable in one place.

- **`kind: 'event'`** — inert flavor: the Article renders on its day (gold-flavored), no countdown, no gate, no coupling to the Chronicle.
- **`kind: 'deadline'`** — a promise that comes to a head by its day. Three states: **Looming** (`currentDay < day`, counting down on the Calendar ribbon), **Due** (`currentDay == day`, the hard gate — see below), **Resolved** (closed early or on-the-day). "Looming/Due" are **pure selectors** over `currentDay` (no stored fired-state). **Many render concurrently** (a ribbon bar per deadline) — no enforced single "active" deadline. Resolution is **outcome-neutral** (prevented / it happened / transformed — the DM narrates); the control says **Resolve**, never "Complete."

**A season is a sparse inherit-forward label** (not a dated Article): set "Late Thaw" on a day and it renders there and every following day until the next season marker (avoids fantasy-calendar complexity).

**Deadline resolution + the one hard gate.** Resolving a deadline writes an **update primaried on the dated Article** (linked agents as concerns), carrying `resolvesAnchor` so it renders as a ⚑ **Chronicle marker** and flips the Article's anchor to **Resolved**; the Article persists as history ("The Eclipse *happened*"), leaves the ribbon (upcoming-only), keeps its timeline. Two entry points, both landing at *{anchor resolved} + {marker written}*: **from the deadline** — a **Resolve** action on the Day-End "LOOMING DEADLINE" alert *and* on the dated Article's page (for early, Persona-style resolution mid-session), which seeds a resolution draft (primary = the Article, prose optional — a bare one-click marker is valid); **from an update** — a "↳ Resolves a deadline ▾" bind control for the "this update *is* the resolution" case. **The hard gate:** the DM **cannot advance past a Due deadline's day while it is unresolved** — the advance is *blocked* (not merely muted) with the reason ("The full moon rises today — resolve *The Eclipse* to advance"). This is the **single deliberate exception to nudge-not-gate**, and it's correct: a due deadline is fiction-certainty — the world moves whether you're ready or not, made literal at its sharpest point. It stacks *above* the soft day-end warning (resolve due deadlines first; then the Cancel / Resolve All / Defer warning governs unresolved beats + empty downtime).

**Bond advancement gates the Lineage Atlas — opt-in, non-breaking.** Each NPC (with a Lineage) carries **one party-wide bond**: a **tier (0–4)** + a count of Collaborator activities *concerning it* from **any** party member. Crossing a **per-tier threshold** (flat **3 completions** in v1) surfaces a **dismissible confirm** ("deepen to \<next tier\>?") — **Confirm** advances the tier; **Not yet** dismisses but **re-surfaces** (on the next Collaborator activity concerning the NPC, and in the Day-End chronicle); the app **never auto-advances**. The confirm surfaces in **two places**: inline on the Collaborator downtime activity, and in the Day-End "Logged today" feed. The **four bond tiers correspond 1:1 to the four Archetype tiers** (Initiate/Adept/Elite/Paragon). The Atlas is a **stateless read-model**, so feeding it a new availability input recomputes for free: in a **gating-enabled** campaign, availability is the **union of two narrative lanes** — the character's **own Origin Lineage** rides the campaign-wide **story tier** (`max(Initiate, story tier)`; the party's shared arc, DM-advanced), and every NPC-held Lineage opens **party-wide** at that NPC's **bond tier** (FR-15); the gate controls **availability only** (**acquisition still spends Saved Ranks**). Implemented by extending the existing gating seam (`hiddenArchetypeKeysFor` is the precedent) with a server-computed availability input passed into `buildLineageAtlas`. **Off**, or for a character not in a campaign → empty input → **all-open as today**.

## Rules Recap

For reference, the mechanics the tool must respect (full text in the Obsidian vault):

**Time, and a moving world (§2.2).** Downtime divides into **activity slots** — two per standard day, each one substantial activity; some take both, or several days. Categories: **Virtue** (a Spark, §1.2), **Collaborator** (an NPC bond, §2.3), **Practical** (crafting/recovery/scouting) — §2.2's three — plus **Talent** (practice toward a Talent, from §2.1), which the composer carries as a fourth tag. **Travel** in **days**. **Deadlines** are fixed in-game dates; usually one active, others looming. And the framing line: *"A campaign moves on whether you do or not."*

**Virtues & Sparks (§1.2).** Virtues are Rank 0–7; **7 Sparks** rank one up. Modeled already — and **owned by the player on their own sheet**; the clock never awards a Spark.

**Archetypes, tiers, the narrative gate (§1.3).** Lineages × four tiers (Initiate / Adept / Elite / Paragon). **The DM gates Lineages/tiers behind narrative progression** (level-gate alternative exists). The app has **no** gate today; this feature adds it via NPC bonds, opt-in per campaign.

**Identity & Origins (§1.4–1.5).** Ancestry/Background, Backstory, **Knives** (~7; external stakes), **Chains** (internal limits), and Identity Traits (Personality, Hopes, Dreams, Fears, Secrets). NPCs reuse this shape as optional, progressively-filled traits.

**Collaborators (§2.3).** Key NPCs whose bonds, deepened through downtime, unlock Archetypes/abilities/story paths; also a Victory source. (Rewards beyond Lineage availability are open.)

**The Arcana Toolkit (DM guide).** Nineteen Major Arcana **NPC blueprints** (Magician–Sun), each a personality + failure mode + arc — the Arcana is the NPC's *soul*, not occupation. **Fool, Judgement, World excluded.**

## User Journeys & States (DM, v1)

- **Author prep (Session Notes).** Create **beats** (title + tagline + prose with entity links) in **sessions** (folders); schedule each to a slot, float it ("run anytime"), or leave it unscheduled.
- **Author the world.** Create **Articles** (name + type + prose) and **NPCs** (name required; Identity/Origins + Arcana + Lineage progressively) — or quick-mint either as a name-only stub from the linker. Wire **relations** between them (directed; bidirectional when symmetric) with free-form labels. Each entity has a page showing its details, its outgoing relations, and its timeline.
- **Plan ahead (Calendar).** Set the **current day** + slot labels; author **deadlines** on future days (optionally pointing at a threat); tag **seasons** (inherit-forward) and **events** (an Article on a day); schedule beats into open slots; extend the horizon with add-days.
- **Run a day (Day Runner).** Walk the slots in order. Run **story beats** (open notes, mark resolved, or **Defer**); on downtime slots, work the per-character workspace — write each character's activity, tag its category, link concerns; adapt live with **Run story beat**. **Collaborator** activities tick the NPC's bond (DM-confirmed). No Sparks/Talents/gold are handed out — the player taps those on their own sheet.
- **End the day (capture ritual).** Review the day's player updates, author **world updates** (tag a participant, write it, link what it concerns) — with **pre-suggested** drafts for resolved beats, fired deadlines, and bond crossings — and **advance** the day. The day-end **warning** guards an unfinished day (Cancel / Resolve All / Defer Unresolved Beats).
- **Look back (Chronicle).** Open the **world timeline**, or any entity's page, to read accrued history. (Past days are the Chronicle's job; the Calendar is upcoming-only.)
- **Open a Lineage in play.** As an NPC's bond crosses a tier, the party's Atlas opens that Lineage's next tier (gating campaigns).
- **Resume / empty states / first run.** Reopening restores exact state; every new surface has an empty state (no clock yet, empty Session Notes, no Articles/NPCs, a quiet Chronicle, a bare Calendar). The Day Runner's empty state is a three-step first-run checklist — **Start the clock** (with a starting-day field, for campaigns adopted mid-flight) → add your first beats → mint the NPCs you already know — and pre-clock Calendar/Chronicle point home. **Deleting a referenced entity** confirms with reference counts; a dangling relation/update reference never breaks a page (resolved: tombstone — see technical-design.md D4).

## Functional Requirements

### 0. Campaign Clock model + lifecycle
A **campaign-scoped clock record** (one per campaign): **current day**, **slot config** (ordered names, default Morning/Evening), optional **season** markers (inherit-forward). Authored and run on **one record** (no template/instance split). DM **starts / advances / un-advances / resumes**; starting takes a **starting day** (a campaign adopted mid-flight mints at day 40). **Advance is reversible — scoped**: un-advance rolls the clock back one day and re-opens any deadline resolved on the undone day; it does not reverse day-end bulk beat actions (deferred beats carry return-to-slot provenance instead — see _Architecture_). Version-guarded; gates on `requireCampaignDM`. No reference to dungeons/encounters; never auto-advances. Writes nothing to a character.

### 1. Day & slot tracking (DM)
Advance an abstract **day counter** (relative math). A day carries **ordered, named slots** (default two; DM renames + adds more). A slot's **kind is derived** (beat scheduled → **story**; dungeon claimed → **dungeon**; both campaign-wide with one "resolved" check; otherwise **downtime**, one free-text entry per placed character). No spanning: no multi-slot/multi-day/party-wide activity as a data shape. Delve/travel is logged as a **time-cost only** (advance N days), DM-chosen; a multi-day advance offers an optional **montage pass** — one free-text entry per character for the skipped stretch, landing on the arrival day — so a time-skip doesn't erase recorded growth. **Past days are frozen**: beats cannot be scheduled into, unscheduled from, or deleted out of slots on days before the current one (history keeps its shape). **Optional free-text labels** rename slots + tag the season; no calendar engine.

### 2. Downtime recording (DM)
A downtime slot holds **one free-text activity per placed character**: prose + a **category** (Virtue / Talent / Practical / Collaborator / **Idle**) + a linked **"concerns" set** (participants). **Idle** is the one-click "did nothing substantial" mark (empty prose legal, muted in the Chronicle and filtered out by default) — recorded per character, or bulk-filled when the day-end warning's proceed buttons accept empty slots. The category is **categorization only** — it colors the Chronicle; **only Collaborator** has a mechanical effect (the bond, §9). No slot cost, no solo/party-wide, no requirement in v1. A **reusable, typed activity library** (named, reusable across days) is **P2**.

### 3. Downtime resolution (no rewards)
Recording an activity **auto-writes an update** on the character's timeline (§8). **Collaborator** activities advance the NPC bond (§9) and write an update *concerning* that NPC. **Virtue / Talent / Practical** activities are logged only. **No Spark/Talent/gold is awarded or offered** — the clock writes nothing to a character; the player taps rewards on their own sheet. All writes gate on `requireCampaignDM`.

### 4. Session Notes & beats (DM)
An Obsidian-style **folder tree**: **folder = session** (purely organizational, no clock coupling), **note = beat** (one beat per note). A beat = title + tagline + prose body with inline **entity chips** + a **`schedule`** (`{day, slot} | floating | unscheduled`). The editor has a **schedule control** (pick a concrete slot / Floating · run anytime / Not scheduled). A note shows its schedule-state icon. Beats can be born here or inline (§1's "New story beat" → default **Unfiled** session). Campaign-scoped; `requireCampaignDM`.

### 5. Day Runner + Defer / Run (DM)
Walk a day's slots in order. **Story slot:** open the beat's notes, **Mark resolved** (auto-advances) / **Reopen**, or **Defer** (→ floating shelf; slot reverts to downtime). **Dungeon slot:** the claimed dungeon's card — **Open dungeon console** (the existing `/campaigns/[campaignShortId]/dungeon/[shortId]`), **Mark resolved / Reopen**, **Remove** (unclaim → downtime). **Downtime slot:** the per-character workspace (roster, vitals glance, composer, one-click **Idle**). **Run story beat** *and* **Run a dungeon** on a downtime slot pull in a beat or claim a dungeon; if the slot has recorded downtime, a confirm sets it aside (kept, reversible). A delve that runs long is extended by **claiming the next slot with the same dungeon** — never enforced, never automatic. **End the day** opens Day-End Capture, guarded by the **day-end warning** (Cancel / Resolve All / Defer Unresolved Beats; empty downtime accepted; never auto-defers).

### 6. Day-End Capture (the ritual)
Review the day's player activities; author **world updates** (tag a primary participant, write it, link concerns) via the composer; **advance** the day. **Pre-suggest** an update for each **resolved beat** (primary = its participant, concerns = its chips, draft = its tagline), each **resolved dungeon slot** (primary-less "the world" update — "The party delved ⟨name⟩"), each **Looming/Due deadline** (primary = the dated Article), and each **bond-tier crossing** — pre-fill only, never auto-write. **The hard gate:** advance is **blocked** while any **Due deadline** is unresolved; the **"LOOMING DEADLINE"** alert offers **Resolve** (seeds a resolution update, primary = the dated Article; prose optional). Resolving flips the anchor to Resolved and writes the ⚑ Chronicle marker (§7). The hard gate stacks *above* the soft day-end warning (FR-5). Advancing is **reversible** (un-advance, FR-0). `requireCampaignDM`.

### 7. Deadlines & Events (dated Articles)
An Article may be **dated**: at most one `{ day, kind: 'event' | 'deadline' }` (a facet of the Article, 1:1 with its date). **`event`** — inert flavor on its day. **`deadline`** — states **Looming → Due → Resolved** (Looming/Due are pure selectors over `currentDay`, no stored fired-state); counts down; **hard-gates** advance on its Due day until resolved (the one nudge-not-gate exception); resolution is **outcome-neutral** ("Resolve," not "Complete"). The **dated Article is the event/threat**; agents (the Demon King) are **linked participants**, never the dated thing; NPCs are **not** datable. **Resolving** writes an update primaried on the dated Article (agents as concerns), `resolvesAnchor` flips the anchor to Resolved and renders a ⚑ Chronicle marker; the Article persists (off the ribbon, timeline kept). Two entry points: **Resolve** on the deadline (Day-End alert *or* the Article page, for early resolution) and a **"↳ Resolves a deadline"** bind on any update. Un-advance re-opens a deadline resolved on the undone day (FR-0). **Re-dating guards:** changing the day of a *resolved* deadline requires re-opening it first, and a ⚑ marker update cannot be re-dated while it binds an anchor (unbind first) — "resolved before it looms" is never representable. (Player visibility deferred — §16.)

### 8. Calendar (DM)
An **upcoming-only** agenda (past = Chronicle). Header: "Now · Day N · \<season\>". A **deadline ribbon** (tick row + a countdown bar per Looming/Due deadline spanning today→due; many render concurrently). An **agenda** of day-cards: each shows season (inherit-forward), any **dated Article** (deadline line or gold **event**) or a "+ Event / + Deadline" affordance (create/pick + quick-create-stub Article, set its date), and its **slots** (story slots show the beat title; dungeon slots the claimed dungeon; open slots offer "+ Schedule a beat" → picks from Session Notes, writing the beat's `schedule` — a delve can likewise be claimed onto a future slot). **Add-days** controls (Add 1 / Add 7 / Add N) extend the planning horizon. Far deadlines **clamp** at the ribbon's edge ("→ Day 74" — the genre-staple deadline is the distant one); beat-scheduling is **day-picker → slot-picker**, not a flat enumeration of every open slot. `requireCampaignDM`.

### 9. Articles (DM)
An **Article**: name, a **type** (curated set + DM-defined custom, **label-only** — filter/icon, no behavior), and free prose. Composes Relatable (§11) + Timelined (§12). **Quick-createable inline** as a name-only stub (name + default type). Campaign-scoped; `requireCampaignDM`. "Faction," "Settlement," etc. are types, not separate entities.

### 10. NPCs (DM)
An **NPC**: **name required**; the PC **Identity/Origins** shape + a Major **Arcana** (toolkit set; Fool/Judgement/World excluded) + an Archetype **Lineage** + the party-wide **bond** — **all but the name optional, filled progressively**. **Lineage is enforced unique per campaign** (one NPC per Lineage — Persona-style, cast from a finite deck; load-bearing for FR-14's unambiguous gate-holder); **Arcana uniqueness is advisory** — the picker warns "held by ⟨name⟩" but allows it, per the DM guide's own counsel ("usually… one Collaborator per Arcana, but don't let it stop you if you have a brilliant idea"). Deleting an NPC releases both back to the deck. A **distinct structured kind** (not an Article type), composing Relatable + Timelined. **Quick-createable inline** as a name-only stub (a valid narrative participant immediately; Collaborator/gating machinery appears only once a **Lineage** is assigned). A **"stub" badge/filter** surfaces under-built NPCs. Reserves a null **statblock/Entity seam** (§15). `requireCampaignDM`.

### 11. Relations (the web)
A **relation** is a **directed edge** `source → target` with an **optional free-form label**, **displayed on the source**. Creating one offers **"also add the reverse"** (writes `target → source` with its own label). Any participant (`article | npc | character`) may relate to any other. Static and authored deliberately; does **not** propagate updates. `requireCampaignDM`.

### 12. Updates & timelines (the Chronicle)
An **update** is **day-stamped**, authored on an **optional primary participant** — none means "the world" (ambient events like *heavy rains wash out the coast road* need no junk Article; they render plainly in the Chronicle and land on no entity timeline) — with free text and a **"concerns" set**. It appears on the primary's timeline (if any) **and** each concerned participant's. Player downtime activities (§3) auto-write updates (always primaried on the character); DM world updates are authored **at Day-End (§6), on any entity's page, or on the Chronicle** — the same composer, mounted where the moment happens, so mid-session events get captured live rather than recalled at the ritual. The static web (§11) does **not** auto-propagate. Beats do **not** auto-log. Updates are **editable and deletable after the day advances** — fix a typo, re-tag, re-date, or delete long after authoring (a ⚑ marker edited/deleted re-opens its anchor; **re-dating a downtime activity detaches it from its slot**, making it a world update, since a slot's day is a fact, not an opinion). `requireCampaignDM`.

### 13. Chronicle surface
A first-class **past-facing** surface: the **world timeline** (all updates), filterable by participant/category — the temporal twin of the Calendar. **Per-entity timelines** (updates where the entity is primary-or-concerned) live on entity pages. Day-End's "Logged today" is the day's slice and **links into** the full Chronicle. *(To be designed before build; behaves as specified here.)*

### 14. Collaborator bond advancement
Each NPC **with a Lineage** carries **one party-wide bond**: a **tier (0–4)** + a count of Collaborator activities *concerning it* from **any** member. Because no two NPCs share a Lineage (FR-10), each Lineage has **at most one gate-holder** — "whose bond opens this Lineage" is never ambiguous. **At most one Collaborator activity per PC per day counts** toward a given NPC's bond — three party members training with Maren the same evening is one deepening moment each, not a tier in a night, and an all-day activity recorded across both slots isn't double-counted. Crossing a **per-tier threshold** (flat **3** in v1) surfaces a **dismissible, re-surfacing confirm**; **Confirm** advances the tier (opening that Lineage tier in gating campaigns), **Not yet** dismisses; never auto-advances. Surfaces in two places (inline on the activity + Day-End feed). Four bond tiers ↔ four Archetype tiers. Bond machinery is **absent on Lineage-less (stub) NPCs**. **Bonds can regress:** relationships sour — the DM can **manually set or drop** an NPC's bond tier (a betrayal, a falling-out), which re-closes the Atlas tiers it had opened. The model is not monotonic; the DM owns the bond.

### 15. Lineage Atlas gating (campaign opt-in)
A campaign setting toggles **narrative Lineage-gating**. **On**: availability is the **union of two narrative lanes** —
- **Origin lane (the party's arc):** a character's **own Origin Lineage** opens at `max(Initiate, story tier)`. The **story tier (0–4)** is campaign-wide, DM-advanced at story milestones (resolving a deadline **pre-suggests** an advance at Day-End; never auto) — the Persona "Fool = the journey" idea: your innate gift deepens as the campaign's story does. Initiate is always reachable; your Origin's higher tiers are story-earned.
- **Bond lane (relationships):** the Lineage held by an NPC opens **party-wide** at that NPC's **bond tier**.

A Lineage is available at the **best tier any applicable lane grants** (so a mid-campaign joiner whose Origin an NPC already holds simply meets the party where it is — never above it); all other Lineages are **locked**. **Availability only** — acquisition still spends Saved Ranks. Implemented as a **server-computed availability input** into `buildLineageAtlas` (alongside `hiddenArchetypeKeysFor`). **Off** / not-in-campaign → empty input → **all-open as today** (non-breaking). Re-derives every read.

### 16. Player-facing visibility & watch (later)
The deferred player layer: the **reveal model** (participant / relation / update switches) and a **redacted player watch** of the clock, deadlines, world-news feed, and entity timelines. v1 is **DM-only/private**.

### 17. NPC-as-combat-enemy (later)
When game-v2 **entity persistence** lands, the NPC's reserved Entity/components seam carries a resolved **statblock** and the DM can **drop the NPC into an encounter** (the same `resolve` that powers PCs). Until then, encounter enemies stay catalog/free-entry.

### 18. Players pick their own downtime (later)
Players choose/propose their own activities for their slots (an owner-action), with DM adjudication.

### 19. Reusable typed activity library (P2)
A DM-authored, reusable activity (name, category, slot cost, solo/party-wide, requirement, description), campaign-scoped, reused across days — the authored counterpart to v1's free-text composer. Deferred to P2.

## Open Questions

> **Resolved 2026-07-07:** every item below marked *(technical design)* — storage realization, dangling references, NPC ⟷ Character reuse, and the Atlas-gating edge cases (highest-bond dissolved by enforced one-NPC-per-Lineage; Origin exemption narrowed to an Initiate floor + story-tier lane) — is now answered in [technical-design.md](./technical-design.md). The genuinely open items remaining: Chronicle layout design, the visibility model, bond-threshold tuning, Collaborator rewards beyond gating, update-authoring weight, Obsidian integration (parked).

- **Architecture / storage realization (technical design session).** Day/slot materialization (computed-with-sparse-overrides vs a day table); the shared `relations` + `updates` tables keyed by `{kind,id}` vs eventually the game-v2 substrate; the clock/update storage split (the update stream grows unbounded → its own table + indexes); day/slot/beat/deadline schemas.
- **Chronicle design.** The surface exists as a decision; its concrete layout, filters, and grouping are to be designed before build.
- **Visibility model (deferred by decision).** How many reveal switches — participant / relation / update — and the redacted player watch. Explicitly **not** designed in v1.
- **Dangling references.** Deleting a referenced NPC/Article → tombstone the label, detach, or block. A dangling ref must never break a page/timeline. *(Technical design.)*
- **NPC ⟷ Character reuse.** Whether NPCs literally reuse the character Identity/Origins tables/components or mirror the shape in their own table; and how this lines up with the game-v2 "PCs and NPCs are both entities" goal + the statblock seam. *(Technical design / v2 roadmap.)*
- **Bond threshold tuning.** v1 ships a **flat 3 per tier**; escalating (Initiate cheap → Paragon dear) or campaign-tunable thresholds are a deferred tuning pass (depends partly on the P2 activity library).
- **Collaborator rewards beyond Lineage gating.** §2.3 also names abilities / story paths and **Victories**; v1 ships only Lineage availability.
- **Atlas gating edge cases.** Origin always exempt; leaving a gating campaign (acquired persist, new unlocks stop); two NPCs sharing a Lineage (**highest bond tier wins**, pending confirmation).
- **Update authoring weight.** Pure free-text + links vs a light optional category, to keep timelines filterable without the form-chore.
- **Obsidian integration (parked).** Evaluated using the now-official headless Sync client (`obsidianmd/obsidian-headless`) to source worldbuilding from a DM's vault. Verdict: a poor fit as the *system of record* — the structured (Lineage/bond → Atlas), temporal (the clock-stamped Chronicle), multiplayer, and redactable parts must stay app-native. If revisited, the shape is a **hybrid**: app owns structure + time; an entity's *prose body* is markdown (the app's existing `components/editor/` primitives), with optional vault-linking as a power-user add-on. Not v1.
