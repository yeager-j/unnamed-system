As mentioned earlier, combat does not take place within a grid system. Instead, the two sides are lined up opposed to each other. However, there is still some flexibility in your placement. There are two Rows available to you: Front and Back. The Row you are in can affect the Actions available to you and attacks coming your way. Before you make your Action, you can choose to move to a different Row. Also, before combat starts, decide which Row you are in.

## Physical Attacks

A combatant in the Front Row that makes a physical attack against another combatant in the Front Row makes their Accuracy check neutrally. If a combatant in the Front Row attacks a combatant in the Back Row OR if a combatant in the Back Row attacks a combatant in the Front Row, the Accuracy check is made with Disadvantage. A combatant in the Back Row that attacks a combatant also in the Back Row will miss their attack.

Some weapons and Skills may function differently. For example, the Riot Gun Skill breaks this rule; even though it’s a Physical Skill, you don’t have Disadvantage when attacking from the Back Row. As a general principle, whenever a Skill conflicts with a rule, always defer to the Skill.

> [!skill] Riot Gun
> Deals severe piercing damage to all enemies in the Front Row. You do not have Disadvantage if attacking from the Back Row.
> 
> HP: **25%**; Damage: **8d6 Physical (Pierce)**; Accuracy: **2**; Critical Hit: **20**

## Magical Attacks

In general, magical attacks don’t care what Row you’re in, but some Skills target enemies depending on their Row. For example:

> [!skill] Maragidyne
> Deals heavy fire damage to each enemy in one Row of your choice, plus half damage to each enemy in the other Row.
> 
> SP: **22**; Damage: **6d8 Magical (Fire)**; Accuracy: **2**; Burn: **17**

Before making your attack, you’d choose a Row to target, Front or Back. All enemies in that Row would be dealt that damage if it hits them. Maragidyne also says that enemies in the other Row take half damage. Affinities are applied per combatant, so take care when using multi-target moves; you may run into a situation where one enemy is Weak to Fire but another Repels it!
