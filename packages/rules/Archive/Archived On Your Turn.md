On your turn, you can Move and take one Action.

## Making an Attack

When you make an attack, you first make an Accuracy check to determine if your attack lands. If it lands, check to see if you are eligible to score a Critical Hit or apply an Ailment. Finally, roll for damage.

### Skill Costs

Nearly every Skill has a cost associated with it. If the Skill lists an SP cost, first deduct that number from your character’s SP. If the Skill lists an HP cost, this will come in the form of a percentage of your **maximum HP**.

![[Cleave#^fb6b38]]

Using Cleave as an example, the Skill would cost **10 HP** for a character with a maximum of **100 HP**. If that character has less than 10 HP remaining, they could not use the Skill.

### Range

The Range of a Skill or weapon determines who you can hit. See [[3.5 Zones & Movement#Range]] for more information.

### Accuracy Checks

Your equipped weapon and each of your Skills have an Accuracy, which represents the minimum roll required to hit your target. Roll a **d20** and then add the difference between your Agility and your target’s. If this total meets or exceeds the Accuracy listed on your weapon or Skill, the attack hits. Otherwise, it misses. A natural 20 always hits and a natural 1 always misses. Rarely, the DM can rule that an attack hits or misses regardless of the roll if the situation calls for it. For example, an enemy that flies high up into the air would be impossible to hit with your sword.

If a Skill does not list an Accuracy, it always hits (unless the DM rules the attack is impossible, like with the above situation).

![[Garu#^3f7b65]]

Using the above Skill as an example, a result of **2 or higher** would hit. A result of **1 or lower** would miss.

> [!info] Optional Rule: Critical Failures
> If your Accuracy check is a natural 1, you also receive the [[Downed]] Ailment in addition to missing.

### Critical Hits

Your equipped weapon and certain Skills may list a Critical Hit threshold. If it does, roll a second **d20** if the attack hits and add the difference between your Luck and the target’s. You score a Critical Hit if the total meets or exceeds the threshold. If a Critical Hit is scored, damage dealt is doubled and the target receives the [[Downed]] Ailment.

![[Brave Blade#^fb6b38]]

Using the above Skill as an example, a result of **14 or higher** would result in a Critical Hit.

> [!info] Optional Rule: Crits from Accuracy
> Some players may be used to D&D 5e’s Attack Roll system where a natural 20 scores a Critical Hit and feel that rolling a natural 20 on this system’s Accuracy check is wasted. If the table as a whole feels this way, you can decide that rolling a natural 20 on an Accuracy check scores an automatic Critical Hit **if the Skill or Weapon lists a Critical Hit threshold**. Make sure to apply this consistently to enemy rolls!
> 
> Note that this will result in 5% more Critical Hits across the board.

### Ailments

Your weapon and some Skills may also apply an Ailment. Similar to Critical Hits, you roll an additional **d20** if the attack hits and add the difference between your Luck and the target’s.

![[Agi#^ed109c]]

Using the above Skill as an example, a result of **16 or higher** will apply the **Burn** Ailment on the target.

> [!info] Speeding Up Play
> You may opt to roll both the Accuracy d20 and the Ailment/Critical Hit d20 at the same time for faster combat. Make sure to keep track of which is which!

> [!info] Optional Rule: Ailments from Accuracy
> Similar to the optional Critical Hit rule, you can decide that a natural 20 on the Accuracy check automatically applies an Ailment.

### Rolling for Damage

Your equipped weapon and each of your Skills also list damage. Roll the dice listed and then add the difference between either your **Strength** or **Magic** and the target’s **Endurance**. If the attack is listed as **Physical**, Strength is used. If the attack is listed as **Magical**, Magic is used. Damage dealt cannot be less than 1.

For example, Joker uses the **Garu** Skill on Mad Marsh Horse. Garu deals magical damage, so Joker’s **Magic** stat is used to calculate damage. If his Magic stat is 10 and Mad Marsh Horse’s Endurance stat is 7, the damage dealt would be **1d8 + 3**. This subtotal is used when multiplying damage when scoring a Critical Hit or hitting a Weakness.

### Basic Attacks

If you perform a Basic Attack, your equipped weapon is used for Accuracy, Critical Hit, and Damage rolls. Basic Attacks have no cost associated with them.

> [!weapon] Katana
> Damage: **1d6 Physical (Slash)**; Accuracy: **2**; Critical Hit: **20**

### Multi-Target Skills

Some Skills can hit multiple enemies. In this case, you’d roll independent Accuracy, Ailment, and Critical Hit d20s for each enemy. However, you only roll damage once and apply that damage to every enemy you hit.

### Multi-Hit Skills

Some Skills can hit multiple times. In this case, you roll independent Accuracy, Ailment, and Critical Hit d20s for each hit and roll damage each time. Some might miss while others might critical hit. For example:

![[Tempest Slash#^368d17]]

### Multi-Hit, Multi-Target Skills

Some Skills can hit multiple times and multiple enemies. In this case, you roll independent Accuracy, Ailment, and Critical Hit d20s for each hit but not each enemy; either it hits everyone or it misses everyone and it either Critical Hits everyone or it doesn’t. Similar to Multi-Target Skills, damage is also rolled once and applied to every enemy for that hit.

|                | Single-Target                                                        | Multi-Target                                                                                |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Single-Hit** | Roll Accuracy, Critical Hit, Ailment, and Damage once                | Roll Accuracy, Critical Hit, and Ailments for each enemy. Roll Damage once total.           |
| **Multi-Hit**  | Roll Accuracy, Critical Hit, Ailments, and Damage once for each hit. | Roll Accuracy, Critical Hit, Ailments, and Damage once for each hit; applies to every enemy |

### Skills with Durations

Some Skills can apply a condition that last for a certain number of turns. The turn counter decrements at the end of the **target’s** turn, **not** the caster’s.

### Skills Targeting Allies

If a Skill says it targets 1 or multiple allies, the caster is included as a valid target.

![[Tarukaja#^452fe8]]

Using this example, you can cast Tarukaja on yourself.

## Communicating

Communication is a free action. You may speak, gesture, or otherwise communicate during your turn without spending your Action. However, the DM may rule that extended negotiation or persuasion requires an Action or is impossible mid-combat. A lengthy monologue may give your enemies an opportunity to act!

## Guarding

If you take the Guard Action, all incoming damage is halved. Additionally, if you are hit by a weakness or receive a Critical Hit, you do not receive the [[Downed]] ailment (described in more detail below).

If you suspect that an enemy is preparing a powerful attack or you are worried they can target your weakness, Guarding might be a good choice. You also might not have anything to do on your turn, in which case Guarding is better than simply passing.
