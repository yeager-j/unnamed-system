Adventuring is exhausting. Between fights, your party will need to bind their wounds, restore their reserves, and catch their breath. Rest is also one of the best opportunities for roleplaying—what does your party talk about over a campfire meal before turning in? How does your character feel after a week sleeping rough in the wilderness, or after a long night staring at the ceiling of a strange inn?

This chapter describes the three kinds of rest available to your party and the consequences of pushing past your limits.

## Full Rest

A Full Rest is eight hours of uninterrupted sleep in a safe, comfortable place after a hearty meal: a bed at the inn, a bunk at your hideout, a guest room at a friend's manor. Full Rests are only available in civilization. If someone has to keep watch, the location isn't safe enough.

Once a Full Rest is complete, your character recovers all HP and SP, regains all spent Hit Dice and Skill Dice, and any temporarily-lowered Attributes return to their normal values. If you have any levels of Exhaustion, it is reduced by 1.

## Partial Rest

A Partial Rest is six to eight hours of sleep in temporary shelter after a decent meal—a campfire and bedrolls under the stars, a hayloft in an abandoned barn, or a cave you've cleared out for the night. The party can take shifts on watch.

After a Partial Rest, your character recovers all HP. Each character may also roll any number of their unspent **Skill Dice** and add the results to their current SP. Skill Dice spent this way are not regained until your next Full Rest.

A Partial Rest is not always available. If the DM rules that the party lacks shelter or a decent meal—if you're stranded in a rowboat in the middle of the ocean, for example—you cannot Partial Rest. You also cannot Partial Rest inside a dungeon (see [[2.2 Time Management#Resting in a Dungeon]]).

If combat begins during a Partial Rest, your party is roused before any of its benefits are gained. Instead, each character benefits from a Respite before the fight starts.

## Respite

A Respite is a brief pause to bandage wounds and catch your breath. Outside a dungeon, a Respite can be taken any time the party isn't in immediate danger. Inside a dungeon, a Respite takes twenty minutes and consumes two dungeon turns.

Each character may roll any number of their unspent **Hit Dice** and add the results to their current HP. Hit Dice spent this way are not regained until your next Full Rest. A Respite does not restore SP.

> For example, Joker has a d10 Hit Die and is at 22 of his 50 HP after a tough fight. He spends two of his Hit Dice during a Respite and rolls a 6 and a 9. His HP is now 22 + 15 = 37.
