Combat begins when one or both sides draw swords and make their intentions known. Not every fight is an honorable duel, though — sometimes one side strikes from the shadows before the other can react.

## Attempting an Ambush

If one side becomes aware of an enemy before the enemy becomes aware of them, that side can attempt an ambush. The ambushing side rolls a **d20** and adds the difference between their side's **highest** Agility and the opposing side's **highest** Agility (this modifier may be negative). If the result is **11 or higher**, or the die shows a **natural 20**, the ambush succeeds regardless of the total. If the result is **1 or lower**, or the die shows a **natural 1**, the ambush fails spectacularly and the other side gets a counter-ambush regardless of the total. Any other result begins combat in a neutral state.

For example, let's say these are the two sides:


|             | Joker | Panther | Skull | Mona |
| ----------- | ----- | ------- | ----- | ---- |
| **Agility** | 4     | 2       | 1     | 5    |


|             | Goblin | Bugbear | Wolf |
| ----------- | ------ | ------- | ---- |
| **Agility** | 3      | 1       | 2    |

The highest Agility on **Side 1** is **5** (Mona) and the highest on **Side 2** is **3** (Goblin), giving Side 1 a **+2** modifier on an ambush attempt. If the d20 shows an 9, the total is **11**, and the ambush succeeds. If the d20 had shown a 8, the total would have been 10 and combat would have begun in a neutral state.

### Successful Ambushes

If the ambush succeeds, the ambushing side takes all of their turns before the opposing side takes any. After both sides have had a full round, normal turn order resumes. If a counter-ambush occurred, the reverse happens — the would-be ambushers go second.

Turns within each side's opening round still follow the drafting rules described in [[3.2 Initiating Combat & Turn Order#Turn Order|Turn Order]] below; the sides simply don't alternate until everyone has acted once.

### Using the Environment

There may be times where the environment is highly beneficial for ambushing, such as a dark corridor with many places to hide. On the other hand, some environments pose a problem for ambushes, such as a bright empty room. The DM may grant Advantage or Disadvantage to the d20 roll depending on the situation, or they may rule an ambush is impossible.

## Turn Order

When combat begins in a neutral state, the side with the **highest Agility** acts first — compare each side's single highest-Agility combatant. If those scores are tied, compare the highest **Luck** on each side instead. If still tied, the DM rolls a d20: on an **11 or higher**, the players go first; on a **10 or lower**, their enemies do.

The side that wins this comparison goes first every round of the encounter. Within each round, turns are drafted one combatant at a time:

1. The side going first picks any one of their combatants to take a turn.
2. The opposing side then picks any one of their combatants to take a turn.
3. Sides continue alternating. A combatant cannot take a second turn in a round until every member of their side has acted.
4. If one side runs out of combatants before the other, the remaining side takes their remaining turns back-to-back to finish the round.

The party chooses collectively which ally goes next. The DM picks for the enemies. Fallen combatants do not take turns until they are revived. New combatants joining the battle mid-round act on their side at the start of the next round.

For example, say Side 1 consists of Joker, Mona, Panther, and Skull, and Side 2 consists of a Goblin, a Bugbear, and a Wolf. Side 2 won initiative. The round might play out as:

**Goblin → Joker → Bugbear → Skull → Wolf → Mona → Panther**

After the Wolf's turn, every enemy has acted, so Mona and Panther take the round's final two turns back-to-back.
