When you successfully Down an enemy by hitting its weakness or scoring a Critical Hit, you immediately take an extra Standard Action (a “Follow-Up”). There is no theoretical limit to the amount of Follow-Ups you can take as long as you keep Downing enemies. However, an enemy that is already [[Downed]] cannot be Downed again, so the practical limit is however many enemies there are. Take care to guard your weakness; enemies can also gain Follow-Ups!

If a character Downs an enemy but also becomes [[Downed]] as a result of that attack (perhaps one enemy was Weak to Fire but another Repels it and the attacker is Weak to Fire, or the attacker rolled a natural 1 on their Attack Roll while playing with Critical Failure rules), that character does **not** gain a Follow-Up.

## Shifting

If you gain a Follow-Up by Downing an enemy, you can pass your Follow-Up to another allied character (called a “Shift”). A chain of Shifts begins when a Follow-Up is first earned and ends when no further Follow-Up is generated. You must do this before moving or taking your Standard Action. You cannot Shift to a character that has an Ailment. After that character has gone, normal turn order resumes (unless they also get a Follow-Up).

You cannot Shift to another character that has already taken a Follow-Up **in the current chain of Shifts**. For example, let's say Joker gets a Follow-Up and Shifts to Skull, who gets another Follow-Up. Skull could **not** Shift back to Joker. Skull decides to Shift to Panther, who receives another Follow-Up. Panther cannot Shift to Joker **or** Skull, since they have already performed a Follow-Up. Once the chain of Shifts ends and normal turn order resumes, Joker and Skull would be eligible to receive a Shift again.

Remember: a Follow-Up only grants you a Standard Action, not a Move Action or a Reaction.

## Showtime!

If you Down an enemy and all other enemies are also [[Downed]] (or if you would have Downed an enemy, but killed it instead), you can choose to immediately call a Showtime! in place of your Follow-Up. Each character on your side makes a damage roll with their equipped weapon and adds their Strength or Magic score, whichever is higher. All enemies in the current combat encounter take this amount of **Almighty** damage. Any enemies still alive after taking a Showtime! are cleared of their [[Downed]] ailment. For example:

> Through a series of strategic Shifts, the party successfully Downs every enemy and it’s Joker’s turn. He can choose to either take his Follow-Up like normal or call a Showtime!. He chooses the latter. Joker, Panther, Skull, and Mona all make damage rolls with their equipped weapons.
> 
> Joker’s St (Strength) is 3 and his Ma (Magic) is 2. His equipped weapon deals 1d8 damage, so he rolls **1d8 + 3**.
> 
> Panther’s St is 0 and her Ma is 4. Her equipped weapon deals 1d6 damage, so she rolls **1d6 + 4**.
> 
> Skull’s St is 4 and his Ma is -1. His equipped weapon deals 1d8 damage, so he rolls **1d8 + 4**.
> 
> Mona’s St is 0 and his Ma is 3. His equipped weapon deals 1d6 damage, so he rolls **1d6 + 3**.
> 
> The combined total for these rolls is **30 Almighty damage**. Every enemy in the combat encounter takes this damage.

You cannot initiate a Showtime! if you are afflicted by an Ailment. Additionally, characters on your side cannot participate in the Showtime! if they’re afflicted by an Ailment (or if they’re Fallen).

After a Showtime!, the combatant next in Agility order takes their turn; no Follow-Up is granted. Enemies controlled by the DM will not call a Showtime! and will instead simply take a Follow-Up, except in very special circumstances.

## Prime Time

Prime Time is your ultimate — a signature Skill unique to your Archetype, and yours alone to call. It’s completely separate from Downing enemies and from Showtime!, and it’s a reward for Shifting a Follow-Up around your entire party instead of hoarding it. If a single chain of Shifts reaches every member of your party during the current turn, the character who receives the Follow-Up at the end of that chain can call their Prime Time in place of their Standard Action.

For example, in a four-person party, Joker gets a Follow-Up and Shifts to Panther. Panther gets a Follow-Up and Shifts to Skull. Skull gets a Follow-Up and Shifts to Mona. Mona has now received a Follow-Up in the same chain of Shifts that already reached Joker, Panther, and Skull — every other member of the party. Mona can call her Prime Time.

![[Elemental Apocalypse#^e72188]]

Where Showtime! is a whole-party effort, Prime Time is your solo spotlight — but the rest of your party still fuels it. Every other party member must pay the Skill’s SP cost, and the targeted enemy or enemies take the combined total damage. If any participant, including the caster, does not have the required SP, the Skill cannot be used. To participate in or use a Prime Time Skill, a character must be healthy; they cannot have an Ailment or be Fallen.

After a Prime Time Skill is used, all enemies who took damage are cleared of their [[Downed]] Ailment.
