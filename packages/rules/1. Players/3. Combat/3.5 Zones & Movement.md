The Zones system is all about theater of the mind. Combat does not take place on a grid, and characters within a Zone are not pinned to a particular spot. Battles flow freely, like the famous duel between Inigo Montoya and Westley, who fight on a precipice one moment and on the rocks below the next without ever leaving their Zone. This system gives you that fluidity while keeping a sense of *where things are* on the battlefield.

## Zones

A **Zone** is a discrete region of the battlefield, roughly 30 feet across. The DM can flex this in either direction depending on the environment. A small alley might be one Zone, while a long hallway might be two or three. A grand cathedral might have one Zone for the nave, one for each transept, and one for the altar.

Two Zones are **adjacent** if a character can travel from one to the other without crossing a third Zone. The DM determines adjacency based on the environment. A staircase, a bridge, or an open archway connects the Zones it joins, while a locked door, a chasm, or a sheer wall might separate them. Before combat begins, the DM describes the Zones in play and where each combatant starts.

## Engagement

At any moment in combat, a character is either **Engaged** with one or more specific creatures or **Free**.

A character who is Engaged is locked in close combat with each creature they are Engaged with, whether knives, swords, or fists. A character who is Free is somewhere in their Zone but not pinned to any single opponent. They have room to maneuver and can act without committing.

Engagement is mutual: if Joker is Engaged with Mad Marsh Horse, then Mad Marsh Horse is Engaged with Joker. A character can be Engaged with multiple creatures at once; a swordsman beset by two enemies is Engaged with both.

A character becomes Free when they are no longer Engaged with any other creatures, whether because they are Fallen, Dead, or have otherwise left the Zone.

## Movement

In addition to your Action, on each of your turns you may **move**. Moving lets you do one of the following:

- **Travel** to an adjacent Zone.
- **Engage** an enemy in your current Zone, becoming Engaged with that creature.
- **Approach** an object or feature in your current Zone if the DM rules you are not close enough to interact with it yet.

Moving is optional, and you may move before or after your Action, but not in the middle of one. You may only move once per turn. Simple environmental interactions like opening a door or kicking over a barrel do not require any of your turn.

### Disengaging

You can break out of an Engagement by taking the Disengage action on your turn. You immediately become Free and can use your Move action to Engage with a different creature, change Zones, or whatever else you like.

## Opportunity Attacks and Interception

At the start of combat, every character has their Reaction available. A character can only use one Reaction per round, so use it wisely; a single Warrior cannot intercept four goblins rushing past them at once.

### Opportunity Attacks

When an enemy you are Engaged with uses their Move action without Disengaging, you can use your Reaction to make a Basic Attack against them with your equipped weapon. The attack resolves before the enemy completes their move. If the attack [[Downed|Downs]] them, their Move action is canceled and they remain Engaged.

You provoke an Opportunity Attack if you use a Skill with a Range other than **Engaged** while you are Engaged. *All creatures you are Engaged with can take this Opportunity Attack.* The Opportunity Attack resolves before the Skill, so a well-timed strike from an enemy might Down the caster before the Skill goes off.

### Intercepting

When an enemy in your Zone uses their Move action to travel to a different Zone and you are Free, you can use your Reaction to **Intercept** them, ending the enemy's Move action and causing the two of you to become Engaged. The intercepting character does not make an attack — they have caught the enemy, not struck them.

## Range

Each weapon and Skill has a **Range** describing where its targets must be relative to the caster. The standard Ranges are:

- **Engaged** — the target must be a creature you are currently Engaged with.
- **All Engaged** — hits all creatures you are Engaged with
- **Same Zone** — the target must be in your Zone, Engaged or not.
- **Same/Adjacent Zone** — the target must be in your Zone or in a Zone adjacent to it.
- **Adjacent Zone** — the target must be in a Zone adjacent to yours, but not in your Zone.
- **All** — can affect all creatures taking part in the same combat encounter limited by Targets.

Skills that reach further than these standard Ranges can specify their reach explicitly:

> **Range:** Up to 2 Zones away.

Any Zone within that distance is a valid target, including the caster's own Zone. A Skill might also specify a minimum and a maximum distance. In this case, the Skill can target the adjacent Zone or a Zone up to 3 Zones away, but not the caster's Zone.

> **Range:** 1-3 Zones away.

A weapon's Basic Attack uses the natural Range of that weapon. Most melee weapons have a Range of **Engaged**. A bow, a pistol, or another ranged weapon will list its Range explicitly.

## Area-of-Effect Skills

Some Skills target multiple enemies at once. AOE Skills come in two forms:

- **Capped AOEs** target up to a fixed number of enemies in a Zone, listed on the Skill's **Targets** line. The caster chooses which enemies within the Zone are hit, up to the cap.
- **Uncapped AOEs** target every creature in a Zone, including allies.

For both forms, the targeted Zone must be within the Skill's Range.

### Casting Recklessly

When a Skill's **Targets** line includes a Reckless bonus, for example: "Targets: 3 (+1 Reckless)", you can declare that you are **casting it recklessly**. The Skill's enemy cap increases by the listed amount.

If any of your allies are in the targeted Zone, casting recklessly comes at a cost: one ally of your choice is also caught in the blast, taking damage and potential side effects alongside the enemies. If the targeted Zone contains no allies, casting recklessly simply hits more enemies with no risk.

Skills without a Reckless bonus cannot be cast recklessly. Uncapped AOE Skills (which already hit every creature in a Zone) likewise have no Reckless option.
