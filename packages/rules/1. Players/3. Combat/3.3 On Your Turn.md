On your turn, you can Move and take one Standard Action.

## Making an Attack

Before you make an attack, check to see if you are in range of your target and then deduct the cost, if any.

### Range

The Range of a Skill or weapon determines who you can hit. See [[3.5 Zones & Movement#Range]] for more information.

### Skill Costs

Nearly every Skill has a cost associated with it. If the Skill lists an SP cost, first deduct that number from your character’s SP. If the Skill lists an HP cost, this will come in the form of a percentage of your **maximum HP**.

![[Cleave#^fb6b38]]

Using Cleave as an example, the Skill would cost **5 HP** for a character with a maximum of **100 HP**. If that character has less than 5 HP remaining, they could not use the Skill.

### Attack Roll

The Attack Roll determines what effect your attack will have. Roll a **d20** and add the listed Attribute score. Then, compare the result to what is listed on the weapon or Skill. If the result is less than the minimum listed on the weapon or Skill, the attack misses. A natural 1 is always a miss. A natural 20 always applies the best result (regardless of totals).

![[Agi#^ed109c]]

If a Skill or weapon lists multiple Attributes, always use your highest for Attack Rolls and Damage Rolls. For example, when using Rebellion Blade, you make your Attack and Damage Roll with either St or Ma, whichever is higher.

![[Rebellion Blade#^fb6b38]]

### Damage Roll

If the Skill or weapon indicates that it does damage as part of the Attack Roll, roll the dice listed next to the corresponding result and add any relevant modifiers, such as your Strength or Magic score. The Damage Roll will also list the damage type, which is important as it interacts with the enemy's [[3.4 Affinities & Damage Types#Affinities|Affinities]].

### Side Effects

Some outcomes have side effects and may list a requirement in parenthesis. If the requirement is an Attribute, compare your score with the target's. If yours is higher, then the side effect is applied. If the target's score is equal or higher, the side effect is not applied. A natural 20 on the Attack Roll automatically applies any side effects.

Some side effects have standard rules and therefore don’t have the requirements listed explicitly.

If a side effect is prefixed with *Auto-*, the side effect applies without an Attribute comparison.

#### Ailments

If the side effect is an Ailment, compare your Luck with the target’s Luck. If yours is higher, the Ailment is inflicted. With the case of Agi, the Burn [[3.7 Ailments, Technicals, & Saving Throws|Ailment]] would be inflicted if your Attack Roll total is 20 or higher and your Luck is higher than the target's Luck.

#### Critical Hits

If the side effect is *Critical*, compare your Luck with the target’s Luck, same as Ailments. If a Critical Hit is scored, damage dealt is doubled and the target receives the [[Downed]] Ailment.

![[Brave Blade#^fb6b38]]

> **Joker**: Nice, I rolled a 13 for my Attack Roll, plus my 3 St for a total of 16. I have 3 Luck.
> 
> **DM**: *Checks the target's Luck score. If it is lower than 3, Joker scores a Critical Hit. The target's Luck was 2 in this example.*
> 
> **DM**: Great, you score a Critical Hit!

#### Insta-Kill

If the side effect is *Insta-Kill*, compare your Luck with the target's Luck, just like Ailments and Critical Hits. If your Luck is higher, the target drops to 0 Hit Points. If the target is Weak to the damage type in parenthesis, ie *Insta-Kill (Light)*, it becomes *Auto-Insta-Kill*. If the target Resists/Nulls/Repels/Drains the damage type, *Insta-Kill* has no effect.

A target is immune to *Insta-Kill* if their Level is greater than or equal to the caster's. Many powerful enemies are naturally immune to Insta-Kill.

### Multi-Target Skills

Some Skills can hit multiple enemies. In this case, you’d roll one Attack Roll and one Damage Roll and apply the result to each enemy.

### Multi-Hit Skills

Some Skills can hit multiple times. In this case, you roll independent Attack Rolls for each hit and roll damage each time. Some might miss while others might Critical Hit. For example:

![[Tempest Slash#^368d17]]

### Multi-Hit, Multi-Target Skills

Some Skills can hit multiple times and multiple enemies. In this case, you roll independent Attack Rolls for each hit and apply the result to all enemies. Similar to Multi-Target Skills, damage is also rolled once and applied to every enemy for that hit.

|                | Single-Target                       | Multi-Target                                                   |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Single-Hit** | One Attack Roll, one Damage Roll    | One Attack Roll, one Damage Roll, applied to each enemy        |
| **Multi-Hit**  | Attack Roll and Damage Roll per hit | One Attack Roll and Damage Roll per hit; applies to each enemy |

### Skills with Durations

Some Skills can apply a condition that last for a certain number of turns. The turn counter decrements at the end of the **target’s** turn, **not** the caster’s.

### Skills Targeting Allies

If a Skill says it targets 1 or multiple allies, the caster is included as a valid target.

![[Tarukaja#^452fe8]]

Using this example, you can cast Tarukaja on yourself.

## Communicating

Communication is a free action. You may speak, gesture, or otherwise communicate during your turn without spending your Action. However, the DM may rule that extended negotiation or persuasion requires an Action or is impossible mid-combat. A lengthy monologue may give your enemies an opportunity to act!

> You sly dog, you caught me monologuing!

## Guarding

If you take the Guard Action, all incoming damage is halved. Additionally, if you are hit by a weakness or receive a Critical Hit, you do not receive the [[Downed]] ailment (described in more detail below).

If you suspect that an enemy is preparing a powerful attack or you are worried they can target your weakness, Guarding might be a good choice. You also might not have anything to do on your turn, in which case Guarding is better than simply passing.
