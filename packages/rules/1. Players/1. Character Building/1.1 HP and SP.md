All characters have Hit Points (HP) and Skill Points (SP). HP represents your physical health; you become Fallen when it reaches 0. Some Skills can cost HP, usually in the form of a percentage of your maximum HP. SP represents your arcane reserves; most Skills cost SP to use.

During character creation, choose one of three paths which determines your Hit Die and Skill Die:

| Path           | Hit Die | Skill Die |
| -------------- | ------- | --------- |
| Health-Focused | d12     | d8        |
| Skill-Focused  | d8      | d12       |
| Balanced       | d10     | d10       |

A martial warrior who expects to take hits might choose the HP path. A mage who relies heavily on Skills might choose the SP path. A well-rounded character might choose Balanced.

## Starting HP & SP

At Level 1, your HP is twice the maximum roll of your Hit Die and your SP is five times the maximum roll of your Skill Die.

|Path|Starting HP|Starting SP|
|---|---|---|
|Health-Focused|24|40|
|Skill-Focused|16|60|
|Balanced|20|50|

## Leveling Up

When you level up, you gain 1 Hit Die and 2 Skill Dice. You may either roll these dice and add the results to your HP and SP respectively, or take the average.

| Path           | Average HP per level | Average SP per level |
| -------------- | -------------------- | -------------------- |
| Health-Focused | +7                   | +9                   |
| Skill-Focused  | +5                   | +13                  |
| Balanced       | +6                   | +11                  |
## Maximum Level

The maximum level a character can achieve is 30.
