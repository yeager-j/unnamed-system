An Archetype represents a collection of skills and passive features that a character can learn. Some systems call this a “Class” or a “Job”. Every player character has at least one Archetype. Player characters can have multiple Archetypes unlocked and available for use, but only one Archetype can be active at once. You can switch between any unlocked Archetypes when you take a [[2.5 Resting & Exhaustion#Respite|Respite]].

Archetypes have a level independent from the character called a Rank, which can range from 1-5. Ranking up an Archetype unlocks new Skills. Reaching the maximum Rank of an Archetype gives your character a permanent bonus to your Attributes, HP, or SP.

## Lineages & Prerequisites

Archetypes are grouped into tree-like structures called Lineages. Higher tier Archetypes in a Lineage require maxing out the Rank of the preceding Archetype. For example, the Wizard is the second-tier Archetype within the Mage Lineage. Unlocking Wizard requires reaching Rank 5 (the maximum Rank) of the Mage Archetype. Some Archetypes may have multiple requirements. The Dark Knight Archetype, for example, requires maxing out its predecessor (the Magic Knight) _and_ reaching Rank 3 of Wizard.

## Affinities

You gain the Affinities of your active Archetype. For example, while the Mage Archetype is active, your character is Weak to Pierce and Resists Ice. Skills or equipment that change your Affinities override the Affinities listed on your Archetype. If you later gain the Null Pierce Skill via Inheritance or from an item, this would override your Weakness.

## Synthesis Skills

Synthesis Skills are powerful Skills used in coordination with your allies. The Combat chapter details the rules for them. Synthesis Skills cannot be inherited.

## Inheritance Slots

Each Archetype has a number of Inheritance Slots. These slots may be filled with any non-Synthesis Skill from any other Archetype you have unlocked, though that Archetype must have the Skill available at its current Rank. The Mage Archetype, being a first-tier Archetype, only has 2 Inheritance Slots.

For example, the Seeker Archetype gains the Tarukaja Skill at Rank 3. If your character has a Rank 3 Seeker, you could give the Mage Archetype the Tarukaja Skill.

## Availability

The DM will often place certain Archetype Lineages or higher tiers of unlocked Archetypes behind a narrative gate. Perhaps to unlock the Mage Lineage, the party must befriend the kingdom’s court wizard. As the players deepen their relationship with the court wizard, higher tiers within the Mage Lineage become available.

While building your character, discuss with your DM to see which Archetypes are available for use and which are gated behind progression.

## Tiers

There are three standard tiers of Archetypes: **Initiate**, **Adept**, and **Elite**. Initiate-tier Archetypes generally have weak Skills that target a single enemy. Adept-tier Archetypes generally have medium-strength Skills and/or weak multi-target Skills. Elite-tier Archetypes have strong single-target Skills and/or medium-strength multi-target Skills. Each tier also provides stronger and stronger passive abilities.

### Paragon Archetypes

The fourth and final tier of Archetypes is the **Paragon Tier**. A Paragon Archetype represents the fullest expression of who your character has become and is the most powerful Archetype available to a player character. Paragons command the strongest Skills in the game and grant the most potent passive abilities of any tier. Each Paragon also possesses a unique Synthesis Skill available nowhere else.

Each character has only **one** Paragon Archetype available to them, determined by the Origin chosen at character creation (see *Creating a New Character* below). A Paragon belongs to the same Lineage as your Origin: a character whose Origin was the Mage Archetype unlocks the Mage Lineage’s Paragon, while a character whose Origin was the Warrior Archetype unlocks a different one. You cannot unlock the Paragon of a Lineage that is not your Origin, even if you have mastered every other Archetype within it.

A Paragon Archetype has two requirements. First, you must satisfy its Archetype prerequisites, which work like the prerequisites of any other Archetype in a Lineage. The Paragon Warrior, for example, might require Samurai 5 and Paladin 5; you must reach those Ranks before the Paragon becomes eligible. Second, you must **break every one of your Chains** (see [[1.4 Character Origins#Chains|Chains]] in the next chapter). Both conditions must be met before the Paragon Archetype unlocks.

Because Chains are broken through meaningful narrative growth rather than mechanical accumulation, unlocking your Paragon is a defining moment in your character’s story rather than a milestone to be ground out. The Archetype prerequisites ensure your character has built the foundation, but breaking your Chains is what gives that foundation its meaning. Once both conditions are met, your Paragon Archetype becomes available immediately; you may activate it like any other unlocked Archetype and rank it up using the standard Archetype Advancement rules.

### Opting Out of Narrative Advancement

The standard rules for unlocking higher-tier Archetypes assume a campaign deeply invested in each character’s personal story. Some campaigns aren’t built that way. A dungeon crawl, a one-shot, or a tactical war campaign may have little room for the slow narrative work [[1.4 Character Origins#Backstory|Knives and Chains]] demand, and that’s a valid mode of play. This section provides an alternate set of gates for groups who want to use this system's combat and Archetype rules without its full narrative apparatus.

Under these rules, higher-tier Archetypes unlock based on character level rather than DM-controlled narrative progression:

| Tier     | Character Level Required |
| -------- | ------------------------ |
| Initiate | 1                        |
| Adept    | 8                        |
| Elite    | 16                       |
| Paragon  | 24                       |

A character may unlock any Archetype of an available tier provided they also meet that Archetype’s standard Lineage prerequisites. A Wizard, for example, still requires Mage 5; the character-level gate replaces only the narrative gate, not the Lineage progression. The Paragon Warrior still requires Samurai 5 and Paladin 5—reaching character level 24 replaces the requirement to break all Chains.

Each character still chooses an Origin Archetype at character creation (see *Creating a New Character* below), and the Origin still determines which Paragon Archetype is available to them. Because the maximum character level is 30, a character who unlocks their Paragon at level 24 has 6 further levels to develop it, master complementary Archetypes, and accumulate Inheritance options.

Knives and Chains remain available as optional flavor in this mode. A DM may keep Knives as a source of dramatic stakes without tying them to the [[1.6 Level Advancement|Victory]] economy, or drop them entirely in favor of milestone-based level progression. Discuss with your group which elements of the narrative system, if any, you want to preserve.

## Advancement

When your character gains a level, you rank up your Archetypes twice; either one Archetype gains 2 Ranks or two Archetypes gain 1 Rank. You do not have to rank up your Archetypes immediately. If you don’t, make a note of how many Ranks you have saved up.

### Creating a New Character

When you create a new character, choose one Archetype available in your campaign setting and rank it up twice. This Archetype is your character’s **Origin**. Your Origin represents the foundation of who your character was when their adventuring life began, and it determines which Paragon Archetype becomes available once you break your Chains—your Paragon belongs to the same Lineage as your Origin.

A Level 1 character begins play with a Rank 2 Origin Archetype and no other unlocked Archetypes.

If you are creating a character above Level 1, first rank up your chosen Origin Archetype twice as described above to establish your Origin, then apply the standard Archetype Advancement rules for each level beyond the first. For each level after Level 1, you gain 2 Archetype Ranks, which you may spend on your Origin’s Lineage or on other Lineages your DM has made available. As with ordinary advancement, you may save some or all of these Ranks rather than spend them immediately. Discuss with your DM which higher-tier Archetypes are appropriate for a starting character of your level.
