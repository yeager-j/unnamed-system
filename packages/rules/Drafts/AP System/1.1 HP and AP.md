All characters have Hit Points (HP) and Action Points (AP). HP represents your physical health; you become Fallen when it reaches 0, and it is the resource you manage across an adventuring day. AP represents your momentum in a fight—the openings, footing, and focus you build as combat unfolds. Nearly every Skill costs AP to use.

Unlike HP, AP does not exist outside of combat. You enter every fight with your Starting AP, build more by fighting, and spend it on Skills. When combat ends, whatever you have left is gone. You cannot save it, stockpile it, or carry it between encounters. Every fight begins fresh.

## Choosing a Path

During character creation, choose one of three paths which determines your Hit Die and your Starting AP:

| Path           | Hit Die | Starting AP |
| -------------- | ------- | ----------- |
| Health-Focused | d12     | 2           |
| Balanced       | d10     | 3           |
| Skill-Focused  | d8      | 4           |

A bruiser who expects to trade blows might choose the HP path and build AP the hard way. A duelist who wants their strongest Skills online early might choose the AP path. A well-rounded character might choose Balanced.

## Starting HP

At Level 1, your HP is twice the maximum roll of your Hit Die.

| Path           | Starting HP | Starting AP |
| -------------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| Health-Focused | 24          | 2           |
| Balanced       | 20          | 3           |
| Skill-Focused  | 16          | 4           |

## Action Points

Your AP ranges from 0 to 9. You begin every combat with your Starting AP. AP can never exceed 9; anything you would gain past the cap is wasted, so pay attention to when you build and when you spend.

### Generating AP

You gain **1 AP** at the start of each of your turns. Momentum builds on its own—the question is how hard you push it.

The dependable way to push is with your weapon. Whenever you make a weapon attack, you gain **2 AP** if it hits or **1 AP** if it misses. Opportunity Attacks count. If you take the Guard Action, you gain **1 AP**.

The fastest engine is the one at the heart of combat itself. When you Down an enemy and gain a Follow-Up, you gain **1 AP**. When a Follow-Up is Shifted to you, you gain **1 AP**. A party that knows its enemies and routes its Shifts well builds momentum exactly as fast as it deserves to.

Your Archetype's unique mechanic bends the AP economy in its own way; playing into your Archetype's fantasy is how you out-earn the baseline.

Finally, entering a fight on your terms pays off. If your side successfully Ambushes the enemy (see [[3.2 Initiating Combat & Turn Order]]), every character on your side gains **2 AP** at the start of combat. If you are Ambushed, the enemy gains the same.

### Spending AP

Skills list an AP cost. Deduct it when you use the Skill. If you can't pay the full cost, you can't use the Skill—there is no casting on credit.

## Leveling Up

When you level up, you gain 1 Hit Die. You may either roll the die and add the result to your HP, or take the average.

| Path           | Average HP per level |
| -------------- | -------------------- |
| Health-Focused | +7                   |
| Balanced       | +6                   |
| Skill-Focused  | +5                   |

Your Starting AP increases by 1 when you reach Level 10 and again at Level 20. Your combat strength otherwise grows through your Archetypes, Skills, and equipment—not through a bigger pool.

## Maximum Level

The maximum level a character can achieve is 30.
