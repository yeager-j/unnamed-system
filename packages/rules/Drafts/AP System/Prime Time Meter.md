Draft replacement for the Prime Time trigger in [[3.6 Follow-Ups, Shifting, & Showtime!]]. The Shift-chain trigger is retired—chains are already heavily rewarded through escalating AP—and Prime Time moves to a shared party meter.

> [!skill] Prime Time Meter
> The party shares a **Prime Time Meter**, which ranges from 0 to 7 and starts every combat at 0. Each time your side Downs an enemy, the meter fills by 1. It cannot fill past 7, and it empties when combat ends.
>
> ***Calling Your Prime Time.*** While the meter is full, any party member may call their **Prime Time** in place of a Standard Action, provided they are healthy (no Ailment, not Fallen). Doing so empties the meter.

^ap7pt1

**Design notes (not rules text):**

- Downs are counted per enemy Downed, whatever the source—Weakness, Critical, Technical. Enemies Downing *you* grant nothing.
- Who calls it is the party's decision at the table. If two players want it, that's a good problem; the DM should stay out of it.
- Monsters do not have a Prime Time Meter.
- Seven continues the system's motif (Victories, Virtue ranks, Sparks, Knives).
- Named literally on purpose: the system's naming budget is spent on currencies whose flavor does mechanical work (Valor, Tells, Forte). This meter's job is "pays for Prime Time," so its name is its documentation. Ratings/Applause/Crescendo stay reserved for future mechanics that can carry the flavor.
